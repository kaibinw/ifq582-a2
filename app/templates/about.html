Our Mission

What We Hold

The Ngurra Digital Library (Ngurra meaning "home" or "country" in many Australian Aboriginal languages) is a university-affiliated academic library based in Brisbane, Queensland, that manages a significant collection of Indigenous knowledge and cultural heritage materials — including oral history recordings, ceremonial documentation, sacred site maps, and archival photographs from communities across Queensland and the Northern Territory.

How We Work

Working alongside a Community Elder Advisory Council, the Ngurra Library is digitising its holdings to ensure long-term while keeping community authority over what is shared and how.

This Platform

The Ngurra web application serves as both a discovery tool and a governance platform, where items remain restricted from public access until approved by the Elder Advisory Council. Each item carries cultural metadata that records its origins, sensitivity, and custodianship, ensuring the collection is handled with appropriate care and respect in alignment to the CARE Principles for Indigenous Data Governance
